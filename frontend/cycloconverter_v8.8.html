Cycloconverter PEM Drive - Key Characteristics

Cycloconverter Operation

  • Direct AC-to-AC conversion (no DC link)
  • Output frequency typically 0-15 Hz
  • Thyristor-based naturally commutated
  • High power capability (20+ MW per unit)
  • Generates significant supply-side harmonics
  • Requires phase-shifting transformer for 12/24-pulse

Synchronous Motor (PEM)

  • Wound rotor with DC field excitation
  • Speed proportional to supply frequency
  • Power factor adjustable via field current
  • High efficiency at rated conditions
  • Requires synchronisation for start/stop
  • Direct drive or single reduction gearbox

Harmonic Filtering

  • Tuned LC filters for 5th, 7th, 11th, 13th harmonics
  • Phase-shifting reduces harmonic injection
  • THD limits per Classification Society rules
  • Filter capacitor degradation monitoring
  • Thermal protection for filter reactors
  • Auto-bypass on filter failure

Typical Applications

  • QM2 (4 × 21.5 MW synchronous motors)
  • Large cruise ships pre-2000s
  • LNG carriers
  • Ice-class vessels requiring high torque
  • Being replaced by VSI PWM on new builds
  • Still operational on legacy vessels
